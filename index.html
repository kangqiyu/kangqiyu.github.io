Research Interest and Biography

I am exploring a new paradigm in machine learning that integrates principles of physics dynamics and mathematics, aiming to address some of the pressing challenges in artificial intelligence, such as effectiveness, interpretability, and robustness. My current work focuses on dynamics-driven continuous graph neural networks, dynamics-guided robust neural networks, and perception-decision practices.

I am currently a Professor (特任教授) at the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC). Before that, I was a research fellow at Nanyang Technological Universty (NTU). I received the B.S. degree in Electronic Information Science and Technology from USTC in 2015, and the Ph.D. degree from NTU in 2020, supervised by Prof. Tay Wee Peng.

Academic services: reviewer for NeurIPS, ICRL, ICML, IEEE TSP, AAAI, CVPR, IEEE TITS, TNNLS, IEEE L-CSS, IJCAI, PR, and others.
